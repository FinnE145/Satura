11. Controls for Do-Not-Track Features

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognising and implementing DNT signals has been finalised. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this Privacy Notice.

California law requires us to let you know how we respond to web browser DNT signals. Because there currently is not an industry or legal standard for recognising or honouring DNT signals, we do not respond to them at this time.

13. Script Content

Users may submit code scripts through the platform as part of gameplay. Scripts are stored on our servers and are only ever accessible to the user who submitted them — other players, including opponents, cannot view your scripts at any time. We recommend that users do not include personal information in their scripts, as script content is stored long-term and could be exposed in the event of a data breach.

14. Account Deletion and Data Retention

When a user deletes their account, their personal data — including their email address, hashed password, and submitted scripts — is permanently deleted. Active accounts retain game history indefinitely. Users may also delete individual scripts at any time through the platform without deleting their account.

15. Shared Game Records

Game records, including move-by-move history, are shared between the two players of each game. If one player deletes their account, their personal information is removed, but the shared game record is retained in anonymised form so that the other player's game history remains intact. Deleted users will appear with a generic, anonymised username.
